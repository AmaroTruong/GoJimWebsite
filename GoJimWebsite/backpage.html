Back Exercises

Lat Pulldown

// gif of demostration // tips on form in a organized list

  1. Sit on the lat pulldown machine with your back against the pad and your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Grasp the bar with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Exhale and pull the bar down towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body.
  4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then slowly release the bar back up to the starting position, inhaling as you lift.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
  8. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

Seated Row

  • Sit on the seated row machine with your feet flat on the footrests and your knees slightly bent.
  • Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your arms extended in front of you.
  • Exhale and pull the handles towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  • Pause briefly at the end of the movement, then slowly release the handles back to the starting position, inhaling as you lift.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  • Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
  • Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

    Single-Arm Dumbbell Row

    1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing each other.
    2. Bend your knees slightly and lean forward, keeping your back straight and your chest up.
    3. Exhale and lift the dumbbells towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your sides and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
    4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly release the dumbbells back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
    5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
    6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
    7. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
    8. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

    Pull-Ups

    1. Grasp the pull-up bar with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
    2. Hang from the bar with your arms fully extended and your feet off the ground.
    3. Exhale and pull your body up towards the bar, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
    4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly release your body back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
    5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
    6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
    7. If necessary, use an assisted pull-up machine or resistance band to help with the movement.
    8. Increase the number of reps gradually over time as you get stronger.
    9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when attempting more challenging variations.
