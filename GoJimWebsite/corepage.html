Core Exercises

Plank

  1. Start in a push-up position, with your hands directly under your shoulders and your feet hip-width apart.
  2. Lower yourself down onto your forearms, keeping your elbows directly under your shoulders.
  3. Engage your core muscles by drawing your navel towards your spine and tightening your abs and glutes.
  4. Hold this position for the desired amount of time, keeping your body in a straight line from your head to your heels.
  5. Remember to breathe normally throughout the exercise, taking deep breaths in through your nose and out through your mouth.
  6. To make the exercise more challenging, try lifting one leg off the ground or alternating between lifting your left and right arms off the ground.
  7. Repeat for several sets, with rests in between.

Tuck Crunch

  1. Start by lying on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground.
  2. Place your hands behind your head, with your elbows pointing outwards.
  3. Lift your shoulders and upper back off the ground, and bring your knees towards your chest at the same time.
  4. At the top of the movement, your elbows should touch your knees or be as close as possible.
  5. Squeeze your abs and hold the position briefly.
  6. Lower your shoulders and legs back down to the starting position, but do not let your head touch the ground between repetitions.
  7. Repeat the movement for the desired number of reps, keeping your form and technique consistent throughout the exercise.
  8. Variation: To make the exercise more challenging, you can try holding a weight plate or a medicine ball between your feet as you perform the tuck crunch.

Cable Crunch

  1. Set up the cable machine: Attach a rope or handle to the high pulley on a cable machine and adjust the weight to a comfortable level.
  2. Kneel in front of the cable machine facing away from the machine, and grab the handle or rope with both hands, holding it at the sides of your head.
  3. Keeping your elbows pointing forward, slowly crunch your torso downwards, squeezing your abs and bringing your chest towards your knees.
  4. Hold the crunch for a few seconds, then slowly release back to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Focus on using your abs to perform the crunch, rather than pulling with your arms or neck.
  7. Exhale as you crunch downwards, and inhale as you release back to the starting position.
  8. Keep your movements slow and controlled, and avoid using momentum to perform the exercise.
  9. Vary the weight and reps to suit your fitness level and goals.
  10. If you experience any pain or discomfort while performing the exercise, stop immediately and consult with a fitness professional.

Leg Raises

  1. Find a sturdy pull-up bar or other hanging apparatus to hang from.
  2. Reach up and grip the bar with an overhand grip, with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Engage your core and lift your legs up towards the bar, keeping them straight and your toes pointed.
  4. Once your legs reach the bar, pause briefly and squeeze your abs and hip flexors to fully engage the muscles.
  5. Slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position, keeping your core engaged and your movements controlled.
  6. Repeat the movement for the desired number of reps, keeping your form and technique consistent throughout the exercise.
  7. If you're having trouble keeping your legs straight, try bending your knees slightly to make the movement easier.
  8. If you're finding the exercise too easy, try adding ankle weights or holding a dumbbell between your feet to increase the resistance.
