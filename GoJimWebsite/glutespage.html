Glute Ham Exercises

Hip Thrust

  1. Sit on the floor with your upper back resting against a bench or a raised platform, and your feet flat on the ground.
  2. Place a barbell across your hips, holding it in place with your hands.
  3. Engage your glutes and push your hips up towards the ceiling, keeping your feet flat on the ground.
  4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then lower your hips back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  7. Use a pad or a towel to cushion the barbell against your hips.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
  9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

Dumbbell Single-Leg Glute Bridge

  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground, holding a dumbbell in each hand.
  2. Extend one leg up towards the ceiling, keeping the other foot on the ground.
  3. Push your hips up towards the ceiling, using your glutes and hamstrings to lift your body.
  4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then lower your hips back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps, then switch legs and repeat on the other side.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  7. Use a bench or a raised platform to elevate your shoulders for a deeper stretch.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
  9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

Seated Leg Curls

  1. Sit on a leg curl machine with your back straight and your feet placed under the padded lever.
  2. Adjust the machine to fit your body and select the appropriate weight.
  3. Curl your legs towards your buttocks by contracting your hamstrings, exhaling as you lift.
  4. Pause briefly at the end of the movement, then slowly release the lever back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  7. Vary the foot position to target different areas of your hamstrings.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over time as you get stronger.
  9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

Hyperextension

  1. Lie face down on a hyperextension bench, with your feet firmly planted on the ground and your upper body leaning forward.
  2. Hold a weight plate or a dumbbell against your chest, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  3. Engage your lower back muscles and lift your upper body up towards the ceiling, exhaling as you lift.
  4. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower your upper body back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  6. Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  7. Use a pad or a towel to cushion your hips against the bench.
  8. Increase the weight gradually over timeas you get stronger.
  9. Use a spotter or safety rails if necessary, especially when lifting heavy weights.

RDL

  1. Start with your feet shoulder-width apart, with the barbell placed on the ground in front of you.
  2. Bend down and grip the bar with an overhand grip, with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Keeping your back straight and your core engaged, lift the barbell up so that it is resting against your thighs.
  4. Hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and your core engaged, and lower the barbell towards the ground while keeping it close to your legs.
  5. Lower the barbell as far as you can without rounding your back, keeping your shoulders back and your chest up.
  6. Once you have lowered the bar as far as you can, raise back up to the starting position by extending your hips and pulling the barbell up with your glutes and hamstrings.
  7. Squeeze your glutes at the top of the movement to fully engage the muscles.
  8. Lower the bar back down to the ground slowly and with control, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  9. Repeat the movement for the desired number of reps, keeping your form and technique consistent throughout the exercise.
