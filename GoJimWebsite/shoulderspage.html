Shoulder Exercises

Seated Shoulder Press

  1. Start by selecting dumbbells of an appropriate weight for your fitness level.
  2. Place the dumbbells on your knees and then lift them up to your shoulders.
  3. Sit with your back straight and feet flat on the ground.
  4. Push the dumbbells up and overhead, fully extending your arms without locking your elbows.
  5. Lower the dumbbells back down to shoulder height, stopping just short of touching your shoulders.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  7. Remember to breathe throughout the exercise, inhaling as you lower the dumbbells and exhaling as you push them up.
  8. End with a cool-down stretch to prevent injury and promote flexibility.

Dumbbell Lateral Raise

  1. Start by selecting dumbbells of an appropriate weight for your fitness level.
  2. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your arms at your sides, holding the dumbbells with your palms facing inward.
  3. Keep your back straight and engage your core muscles.
  4. Slowly lift the dumbbells out to the sides, keeping your arms straight and your elbows slightly bent.
  5. Stop when the dumbbells reach shoulder height, making sure to keep them parallel to the ground.
  6. Hold the position for a second, then slowly lower the dumbbells back to your sides.
  7. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  8. Remember to breathe throughout the exercise, inhaling as you lift the dumbbells and exhaling as you lower them.
  9. End with a cool-down stretch to prevent injury and promote flexibility.

Cable Lateral Raise

  1. Start by attaching a single-handled stirrup to the low pulley cable of a cable machine.
  2. Stand sideways to the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart and the handle in the hand farthest from the machine.
  3. Keep your back straight and engage your core muscles.
  4. Slowly lift the handle out to the side, keeping your arm straight and your elbow slightly bent.
  5. Stop when the handle reaches shoulder height, making sure to keep it parallel to the ground.
  6. Hold the position for a second, then slowly lower the handle back to your side.
  7. Repeat for the desired number of reps, then switch sides and repeat the exercise with the other arm.
  8. Remember to breathe throughout the exercise, inhaling as you lift the handle and exhaling as you lower it.
  9. End with a cool-down stretch to prevent injury and promote flexibility.

Dumbbell Rear Delt Row

  1. Start by selecting dumbbells of an appropriate weight for your fitness level.
  2. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent.
  3. Hinge forward at your hips, keeping your back straight andyour core engaged.
  4. Hold the dumbbells with your palms facing each other and let them hang down in front of you.
  5. Slowly lift the dumbbells up and out to the sides, keeping your elbows slightly bent and your palms facing down.
  6. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
  7. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  8. Remember to breathe throughout the exercise, inhaling as you lift the dumbbells and exhaling as you lower them.
  9. End with a cool-down stretch to prevent injury and promote flexibility.
